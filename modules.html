Modules

Below are the modules I have taken in my BSc (Hons) Computer Science degree and what I have learned:

Honours Year Computer Science Project

In this module, I worked independently on an extended project under the guidance of an academic supervisor. This project consolidated my learning from the taught part of the course and involved authentic assessment designed to encourage the communication of complex ideas through various media. By the end of the module, I developed proficiency in many skills and gained confidence with enhanced skills in clear and effective communication.

Software Engineering 2

I was introduced to a range of advanced, near-research level topics in contemporary software engineering. The course covered issues from both a user-driven perspective—where new software problems emerge—and a technology-driven perspective—where new technologies create opportunities for software products. I engaged with software engineering research articles and participated in seminar-style discussions to deepen my understanding of these advanced topics.

Optimisation

I took an in-depth look at optimization methods applied to various optimization models. These methods are widely used in both academic research and industrial practices, providing valuable techniques for solving complex problems.

Computer Vision

I was introduced to Computer Vision and developed practical skills for building computer vision applications. I explored fundamental problems in both 2D and 3D vision and studied a range of classical and emerging approaches to address these challenges.

Cloud Computing

I received an introduction to cloud computing, covering both the physical infrastructure (such as data centers, networks, and servers) and the software stacks that operate on it (including containers, micro-services, orchestration, and web frameworks). As part of the course, I assembled a cloud-based application, specifically a webpage with a scalable micro-service-based backend as shown here.

Robot Perception & Manipulation

I focused on how robots perceive the world and perform manipulation tasks, relevant to applications like warehouse robots and assistive robots in domestic settings. I studied how sensory data, such as visual and tactile information, is converted into representations like object shapes, poses, and textures. These representations are crucial for effective grasping and manipulation of objects.

Computer Forensics

I studied Forensic Computing, which involves examining and analysing data from various computer storage mediums to be used as evidence in court. I developed the skills and knowledge needed to conduct a forensic investigation systematically, using established methods, tools, and techniques.

Software Engineering 1

In this module, I addressed the various issues related to the analysis, design, implementation, and testing of significant computing systems. It provided me with a comprehensive understanding of the complexities involved in developing large-scale computing systems.

Database Development

I was introduced to various problems related to databases, such as concurrency issues and information security considerations, and learned how these problems are addressed. I explored the integration of heterogeneous information sources and the use of semi-structured data, as well as non-relational databases and the economic factors influencing their selection. Additionally, I studied techniques for analyzing large amounts of data, including the associated security issues and commercial considerations.

Planning your Career

I gained a deeper understanding of crucial employability skills necessary for securing a placement or a graduate job. It focused on enhancing my practical skills and strategies to improve my chances in the job market.

Complexity of Algorithms

I studied techniques like dynamic programming and recursion for designing and analyzing algorithms and data structures. I tackled fundamental algorithmic problems, including searching, sorting, and network flows, and learned about efficient algorithms to address these issues. The module emphasized problem-solving with efficient algorithms, focusing on their formal analysis and implementation, which helped expand my skills for effective programming.

Group Software Project

In this module, I had the unique opportunity to complete a substantial software development project as part of a team. This experience allowed me to apply my skills in a collaborative setting and tackle real-world software development challenges.

Computer Networks

I was introduced to current computer networks and communications technologies. I focused on the architecture and protocols of the Internet to understand fundamental networking concepts. This included an in-depth study of key protocols that facilitate Internet communication, familiarization with various network devices and addressing schemes, and identification of critical network security issues along with approaches to address them.

Advanced Artificial Intelligence

I was introduced to machine learning, covering traditional algorithms, deep learning techniques, and probabilistic graphical models. The module provided both theoretical knowledge and practical skills in these areas, equipping me with a solid foundation in machine learning.

Cyber Security

I received a thorough introduction to Cyber Security, covering topics such as cryptographic algorithms and protocols, system vulnerabilities and attacks, as well as computer networks and web security. The lectures presented the fundamental concepts and theoretical foundations, while extensive practical sessions helped me develop hands-on skills in cybersecurity.

Scripting Languages

In this module, I covered two scripting languages: JavaScript and Python. The course provided a deep dive into scripting concepts, including their syntax and application in various scenarios. This experience allowed me to develop proficiency in writing scripts for different programming tasks.

Design System for Digital Society

This module involved exploring the design and development of systems that support digital society functions. I learned about user experience design, interaction design, and system architecture. The course emphasized creating user-centered designs that enhance the usability and effectiveness of digital systems.

Foundations of Computer Science

I gained a comprehensive understanding of fundamental computer science concepts, including basic programming, algorithm design, and problem-solving techniques. This module laid the groundwork for more advanced studies in the field.

Intro to Artificial Intelligence

I was introduced to the field of Artificial Intelligence, covering essential topics such as search algorithms, knowledge representation, and reasoning. The course provided an overview of AI techniques and their applications.

Data Structures & Algorithms

I studied various data structures and algorithms, focusing on their design, implementation, and analysis. This module equipped me with the skills needed to efficiently handle and manipulate data in various computational scenarios.

Analytic Techniques

The course introduced me to a range of analytic techniques used in data analysis and problem-solving. I learned methods for interpreting data and applying statistical and computational techniques to derive insights.

Object-Oriented Programming

I explored object-oriented programming concepts, including classes, objects, inheritance, and polymorphism. This module provided a solid foundation in designing and implementing software using object-oriented principles.

Computer Systems

I gained an understanding of computer systems' architecture, including hardware components, operating systems, and system-level programming. This module helped me understand the underlying structure and functioning of computer systems.

Programming Language Paradigms

The course covered various programming paradigms, such as procedural, functional, and logic programming. I learned about different programming approaches and their advantages and applications in solving diverse computational problems.
